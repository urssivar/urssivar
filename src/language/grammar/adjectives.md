# Adjectives

The main thing you should know about Kaitag adjectives is their positioning and predicate role.

When adjectives preced the main word, they can and usually are given in short, pure form:

- ** ҡяр** "a sweet pear"
- **итӏин жуже** "a red flower"

When adjectives serve as the head of sentence or clause, they must have suffix that turns them into nouns.

- **Ьеж жуже ччакко==це== це.** "the flower is beautiful."
- **Ьеяр бицци==те== це.** "The pear are tasty."

The same positional logic applies to the three verbal participles (**-ив**, **-иж**, **-ан**) that can act as adjectives:

- **Убал хурег барив.** "Mother prepared a meal."
- **убал барив хурег** "the meal prepared by mother"
- **Рирси рулчӏан.** "The girl studies."
- **рулчӏан рирси** "a studying girl"

Similarly, some nouns can act as adjectives when placed in preceeding positions:

- **баьадур мургул**
  "a heroic man"
- **мургул баьадур де**
  "the man was a hero"
- **авдал уршбе**
  "stupid guys"
- **уршбе авдалте де**
  "the guys were idiots"

## Agreement

The adjective must agree with class and number.

For singular, the suffix is **-це** when adjective ends with a vowel and is **-ил** when it ends with a consonant. For plurals the suffix is always **-те**.

- **бяъ буҡу==це== це**
  "the wall is yellow"
- **бяъ шиниш==ил== це**
  "the wall is green"
- **бяъруме шиниш==те== це**
  "the walls are green"

Some adjectives have class slot that must obey the general class-person agreemet rules:

- **==б==уцӏар бар** "hot day"
- **==д==уцӏар бирме** "hot days"
- **Ьештти дул чи==д==ажив==те== аччу** "These are not the ones that I've seen (i.e. seen by me)."

## Conjugation

The marked ejectives are effectively nouns and can receive cases. The oblique case for **-це** is **-чи-**, and for **-те** it's the usual **-та-**:

- **шинишил==ла== баьа**
  "the price of the green one"
- **шиништа==ла== баьа**
  "the price of the green ones"
- **никӏочи==й== беччив**
  "gave it to the little one"
- **никӏота==й== беччив**
  "gave it to the little ones"

## Adverbs

Any adjective can be turned into adverb by suffix **-ле**, shortened to **-л** after vowels. The adverbs usually come before verbs or copulas

- **ччакко==л== баривде**
  "you did it beautifully"
- **ттура гуна==л== де**
  "[it was] warm outside"
- **ҡяр музи==л== це**
  "the pear is [tasting] strongly sweet"

The preterit verbal participles also can be turned into adverbs, in which case they act as perfective forms. They have special reduced forms — **-ив** → **-ил**, **-ур** → **-ул**, **-ун** → **-ун** — and their stress always moves to the start of the word.

- **убал хурег барил це**
  "mother has prepared meal"
- **атта цағил де**
  "father had arrived"
